Questions homeowners ask

Get comfortable before you book the inspection.

Do I need a repair or full replacement?

It depends on the age of the roof, leak source, shingle condition, ventilation, and how widespread the damage is. An inspection helps compare both options.

How fast can someone inspect a leak?

Urgent leak requests are prioritized, especially during active weather. Same-week inspection availability is highlighted when scheduling allows.

Do you provide written quotes?

Yes. The demo positions the contractor around clear written quotes, scope notes, and photos so homeowners understand the recommendation.

Can you repair eavestroughs, soffit, and fascia too?

Yes. Exterior repair leads are included because drainage and roof-edge issues often connect to roofing problems.

Do you work on flat roofs?

Flat roofing inspections and repairs are included for low-slope sections, additions, garages, and similar roof areas.

What happens during a roof inspection?

The crew checks visible roof surfaces, flashing, vents, valleys, drainage, roof edges, and problem areas shared by the homeowner.

Do you clean up after the job?

The trust section includes a clean jobsite promise, which is important for roofing projects with nails, packaging, and old materials.

Is there a workmanship warranty?

Yes, the fictional company offers a workmanship warranty as a credibility signal without claiming sensitive third-party certifications.
