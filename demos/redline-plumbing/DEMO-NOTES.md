# Redline Plumbing & Drains Demo Notes

Use these notes when explaining the site to a trades business owner.

## The Big Idea

This site is built like a money tool, not a brochure. The page assumes the visitor has a plumbing problem, is probably on a phone, and wants the fastest trustworthy next step.

## Conversion Features

**Always-visible phone number**

The phone number is in the header and the mobile sticky call bar. A homeowner with a burst pipe should never need to open a menu or hunt for the number.

**Problem-led hero**

The headline starts with the customer's problem: "Burst pipe? Blocked drain?" That is stronger than a generic "Welcome to our plumbing company" message because it matches the visitor's urgent search.

**Above-fold quote form**

On desktop, the form is visible immediately beside the hero. On mobile, it appears right after the hero. This captures people who are not ready to call but will still request help.

**Short form**

The form uses five fields: name, phone, suburb, problem, preferred time. Long forms create friction. This version gives the business enough information to call back and book.

**Emergency banner**

The red emergency strip creates a second clear phone path for urgent jobs. It reinforces that emergency service is available 24/7.

**Trust strip**

Licensed & Insured, 15+ Years, Locally Owned, and Upfront Pricing answer the main objections before they slow the visitor down.

**Service cards**

The six cards let people self-identify their problem quickly: emergency plumbing, drain cleaning, water heaters, leak repair, fixture installation, and sump pumps.

**Reviews block**

The reviews are short, local, and specific. Names plus suburbs make them feel more believable than vague praise.

**Service area section**

The city list helps visitors confirm that Redline serves their area. It also supports local SEO for Hamilton, Stoney Creek, Ancaster, Dundas, and Burlington.

**Repeated final CTA**

After the visitor has seen services, trust points, reviews, and service areas, the final section gives them the same two choices again: call or request a quote.

**Footer NAP**

Name, address, and phone appear in the footer for trust, local SEO consistency, and quick reference.

**Google Business Profile button**

The placeholder GBP link is there because many trades customers use reviews and map listings to validate a company before calling.

**Click tracking**

Every phone click and form submit is tracked with simple event names. This lets the business see which parts of the site generate calls and quote requests once analytics is connected.

## How To Explain The Value

The site removes three common leaks in trade websites:

1. The phone number is hard to find.
2. The site talks about the company before it talks about the customer's problem.
3. There is no clear backup option for people who want a quote but do not want to call.

This demo fixes those leaks with a visible phone number, urgent service messaging, a short form, local proof, and repeated calls to action.
