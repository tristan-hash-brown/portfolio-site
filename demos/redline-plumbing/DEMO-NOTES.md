# Redline Plumbing — Demo Notes: How This Site Gets You More Quote Requests

*Use this in sales conversations to explain the conversion strategy behind each feature. Every decision has a reason tied to how trades customers behave on mobile.*

---

## The Core Premise

Most plumbing websites look like digital business cards. They tell you the company exists. They don't urgently ask for your business.

A homeowner with a burst pipe at 10pm isn't browsing — they're panicking. Every element of this site is designed to intercept that moment and turn it into a phone call or a submitted form before they hit the back button.

---

## Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

### 1. Sticky Mobile Call Bar

**What it is:** A fixed red bar pinned to the bottom of every screen on mobile that says "📞 Call Now — (905) 555-0142."

**Why it works:** On mobile, the header scrolls out of view within seconds. A homeowner 800px into reading your services page no longer has the phone number in front of them. The sticky bar solves this permanently — the call option is always one tap away, regardless of where they are on the page.

**The detail:** It only appears on mobile where the header phone is hidden. On desktop the header phone is always visible so the bar isn't needed — and it would waste screen space.

---

### 2. Quote Form Above the Fold (Desktop)

**What it is:** On desktop, the quote form sits in a card to the right of the hero headline — visible without scrolling.

**Why it works:** A user who arrives and doesn't want to call still needs an easy next step. The two actions — call or fill out a form — are both present the moment the page loads. There's no hunting. The longer someone has to look for how to contact you, the more likely they leave.

**Mobile behaviour:** On mobile the form appears immediately below the hero text, still the first thing a user reaches after reading the headline.

---

### 3. Problem-Focused Headline

**What it is:** "Burst pipe? Blocked drain? We're on it today."

**Why it works:** Generic headlines like "Professional Plumbing Services in Hamilton" tell the customer what you do. They already know — they searched for a plumber. This headline reflects their situation back at them, confirming they're in the right place. "Today" adds urgency and sets an expectation of speed, which is exactly what emergency trades customers are buying.

---

### 4. Emergency Banner

**What it is:** A red banner immediately below the header: "24/7 Emergency Service — Burst pipe? Don't wait."

**Why it works:** Many visitors arrive after searching "emergency plumber Hamilton." The banner confirms within half a second that this company handles emergencies — before they've read a word of copy. It reduces bounce rate for the highest-intent visitors.

---

### 5. Trust Strip

**What it is:** ✓ Licensed & Insured · ✓ 15+ Years in Business · ✓ Family Owned & Local · ✓ Upfront Pricing

**Why it works:** Homeowners letting a tradesperson into their home need to quickly answer two questions: "Are they legitimate?" and "Will they rip me off?" The trust strip addresses both in a single scan.

**Upfront Pricing** specifically addresses the #1 objection tradespeople face: fear of hidden costs. Naming it explicitly removes a hesitation that would otherwise stop someone from calling.

---

### 6. Five-Field Form (Not Twelve)

**What it is:** Name, phone, suburb, problem description, preferred time. That's it.

**Why it works:** Every additional field in a form reduces completion rate. Trades customers on mobile, often in a stressed situation, will abandon a long form. Five fields captures everything the business needs to make the callback useful — and nothing more.

**Suburb as a dropdown** (not a text field) removes typing friction and lets the office prioritise callbacks by area and confirm coverage before calling back.

---

### 7. Reviews with Suburb Attribution

**What it is:** Three reviews, each attributed as "First Name, Last Initial — Suburb."

**Why it works:** Generic testimonials ("Great service! — John") feel fabricated. Suburb attribution adds geographic specificity that makes reviews feel real and locally verified. A potential customer in Stoney Creek reading "Mike R., Stoney Creek" makes a direct connection — this company has worked in my neighbourhood.

---

### 8. Service Area Pills (Linked)

**What it is:** Clickable area pills in the home page service area section, each linking to the relevant city section on the areas page.

**Why it works:** Without this, a visitor in a suburb not prominently listed may assume they're not covered and leave. Linking each city pill to a dedicated section with response time info confirms coverage and builds confidence before the call.

---

### 9. Response Time Badges on Areas Page

**What it is:** "⚡ 30–45 min response" badges on each city section.

**Why it works:** Response time is the #1 decision factor for emergency plumbing. Showing it explicitly on the areas page removes a question the visitor would otherwise need to ask — and answers it favourably before they've even picked up the phone.

---

### 10. Services Page — Detail + Photo + Dual CTA Per Service

**What it is:** Each service gets its own full section with a description, a specific bullet list, a photo placeholder, and two CTAs (Call + Request Quote).

**Why it works:** A homeowner searching "water heater repair Hamilton" may land directly on the services page. The detail section gives them enough information to confirm this is their problem and Redline handles it, then immediately presents both conversion paths — call now or request a callback.

**The dual CTA matters:** Phone-ready visitors call. Hesitant visitors fill out the form. Offering both captures both.

---

### 11. About Page — Stats + Credentials + Why-Us Grid

**What it is:** Stat boxes (15+ years, 3,200+ jobs, 4.9★), a credentials list (licensed, TSSA, insured, BBB), and a six-card "why us" grid.

**Why it works:** Homeowners visit About to confirm legitimacy, not to read a biography. The stat boxes communicate scale and track record in three numbers. The credentials list answers "are they actually licensed?" The why-us cards handle the remaining objections — pricing surprises, late arrivals, messy work — before the customer has to ask.

---

### 12. Contact Page — Emergency Nudge

**What it is:** A red-bordered nudge above the form: "🚨 Emergency? Call (905) 555-0142 — we answer 24/7."

**Why it works:** Someone with a burst pipe who lands on the contact page should not be filling out a form — they should be calling. The nudge catches that scenario and redirects them to the phone before they waste time on a form that won't get them help fast enough.

---

### 13. In-Page Form Success (No Redirect)

**What it is:** When the form is submitted successfully, the form hides and a success message appears in its place — on the same page, no redirect.

**Why it works:** Redirecting to a thank-you page loses the visitor. They may navigate away before reading anything else. Keeping them on the page after submission means the phone number, reviews, and trust signals are still visible — reinforcing the decision they just made and making it easy to call if they decide they want a faster response.

---

### 14. JSON-LD Schema on Every Page

**What it is:** Machine-readable structured data on every page describing the business — name, phone, address, hours, services, service areas.

**Why it matters:** This is what powers Google's local Knowledge Panel, enables star ratings in search results, and helps the site appear in "plumber near me" searches. It's invisible to visitors but directly affects how many arrive in the first place.

---

## How To Use This In a Sales Conversation

*"Most plumbing websites look fine on a desktop in a design agency. This one is built for a 50-year-old homeowner on their phone at 11pm with water coming through their kitchen ceiling.*

*The phone number never disappears. The form is always one scroll away. Every review says where the customer lives. Response times are on the areas page. The contact page tells emergency callers not to fill out a form. The services page gives every service its own section with a photo and two ways to take action.*

*That's the difference between a site that looks good and a site that makes the phone ring."*
